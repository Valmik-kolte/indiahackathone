Ancients Of India

Ajanta Caves

Rock-cut Buddhist cave monuments dating back to the 2nd century BCE in Maharashtra, India.

Ellora Caves

Monolithic rock-cut temples representing Buddhism, Hinduism, and Jainism.

Nalanda University

One of the world's first residential universities and a center of learning in ancient India.

Golden Temple

The Golden Temple in Amritsar is the holiest shrine of sikhism, Known for its spritual significance and golden architecture

Raigad Fort

It stands 2700 feet above sea level and was the site of his coronation in 1674. The fort includes royal palaces, the king’s court (Raj Sabha), Jagadishwar Temple, and Shivaji Maharaj’s Samadhi. It symbolizes Maratha pride, courage, and architectural excellence.

Shivrai Mudra

During the time of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj (17th century), the coinage was influenced by the Mughal and Adilshahi empires. When Shivaji Maharaj established his own independent Swarajya, he introduced his own coins.

Grishneshwar Temple

Located in Verul village near Ellora Caves, Maharashtra, Grishneshwar is the 12th Jyotirlinga of Lord Shiva. Originally built by Maloji Bhosale and later restored by Ahilyabai Holkar, the temple is a fine example of Hemadpanthi architecture.

Maurya Empires

Founded by Chandragupta Maurya, it was one of the largest empires in Indian history. Emperor Ashoka spread Buddhism after the Kalinga war and promoted peace and Dharma through his edicts.

Gupta Empire

The Gupta Empire (c. 320–550 CE) is considered the Golden Age of India due to its remarkable achievements in science, mathematics, astronomy, religion, and literature. Scholars like Aryabhata made advances in algebra and planetary models, while Kalidasa enriched Sanskrit literature.

Satavahana, Chola, Pallava, Kushan

These dynasties contributed greatly to Indian architecture, maritime trade, and temple construction. Cholas were famous for Brihadeeswarar Temple, and Kushans for promoting Gandhara art.

Takshashila University

Takshashila was an ancient seat of learning that attracted scholars from across Asia. It wasn’t a university in the modern sense but a hub of knowledge with multiple teachers and disciplines.

Ancient Scripts & Languages

Ancient India had a rich tradition of written and spoken languages. Scripts and languages developed for religion, trade, governance, and literature.
